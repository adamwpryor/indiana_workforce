### 2. Discussion Narrative & Decision Log

#### **Lilly Foundation AI Initiative for Indiana Schools**

* **Initial Context:**
  Adam Pryor was unexpectedly brought into a major initiative where the Lilly Foundation is distributing $500M to Indiana schools for AI projects, with minimal prescriptive guidance. The foundation is convening 30 schools for a rapid, two-day working conference to ideate both internal and collaborative projects. CIC (Council of Independent Colleges) was invited to help lead programming, with Adam focusing on higher education and workforce intersections.
* **Options & Perspectives:** Adam initially assumed industry partners would drive the workforce side, but found their AI/workforce expertise lacking. He identified a need for practical frameworks and case studies to help colleges understand and plan for AI integration, referencing Krystal Rawls‚Äô work as a model. Krystal offered her expertise in workforce development, AI training, and case study design, emphasizing a three-level approach: (1) prompt-level operational AI, (2) workflow automation, and (3) agentic operations (not recommended for higher ed yet due to security concerns). Both agreed on the importance of practical, low-lift, high-ROI solutions and the need for discernment skills over mere AI literacy.
* **Final Decision & Rationale:** The group decided to focus on developing and sharing actionable case studies and frameworks, with Krystal providing her materials and Adam tailoring them for the Indiana context. The rationale is to equip colleges‚Äîespecially those with low AI sophistication‚Äîwith concrete, discussion-ready examples that can drive both internal and cross-institutional planning, while avoiding overreliance on vendor solutions or risky automation.

#### **Workforce Development & AI Integration Models**

* **Initial Context:**
  Krystal described her grant-funded model for embedding students in local workforce ecosystems, using labor market data (e.g., Lightcast), student choice, and community partnerships to close the digital divide and provide real-world experience. She highlighted the lack of work colleges on the West Coast and her efforts to launch a Civic Institute/work college model.
* **Options & Perspectives:** The team discussed the trade-offs between technical upskilling (e.g., code literacy) and focusing on durable, transferable skills (analytics, project management, marketing). Krystal advocated for minimal technical requirements for faculty, embedding small, scalable technical modules (e.g., 8-hour LinkedIn Learning pathways) into curricula, and prioritizing student empowerment and choice in workforce placements. Adam noted the potential to adapt this model for both urban and rural colleges, with different resource needs (e.g., housing vs. digital infrastructure).
* **Final Decision & Rationale:** The consensus was to prioritize models that are student-centered, scalable, and adaptable to local needs, leveraging existing community and industry partnerships. The rationale is to maximize impact with limited resources, ensure student agency, and avoid commodification of AI education.

#### **Barriers to Implementation & Funding**

* **Initial Context:**
  Krystal shared challenges in securing executive sponsorship and funding for her work college model, despite demonstrated success and alignment with institutional missions. Leadership turnover and resource constraints at CSU and other institutions were cited as major barriers.
* **Options & Perspectives:** Adam suggested the possibility of integrating the model as a specialized college or workforce lab within existing structures, and discussed the need to align with accreditation requirements. Both acknowledged the difficulty of launching new programs in the current funding climate.
* **Final Decision & Rationale:** No immediate solution was reached, but both agreed to continue exploring funding and partnership opportunities, with Adam offering to help with backend analytics and grant writing as needed. The rationale is to keep momentum and be ready to act if/when funding or leadership support materializes.
