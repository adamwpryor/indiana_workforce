# Cross-Project Synthesis: Framework Integration for Lilly Foundation Convening

**Purpose:** To synthesize the core narrative elements from previous presentations (AAUA Keynote, Presidents' Institute Panel, and Goodwill Workforce Prompt) into a unified strategic foundation for the Lilly Foundation convening ("Preparing Students for the Workforce, AI and Collaboration").

## 1. The Core Narrative Arc

The Lilly session must weave together three distinct threads from our previous work:

- **The Existential Context (From AAUA):** Higher education is facing a "Perfect Storm" (Resistance, Model Ending, Fading Credentials) and a "Student Paradox" (near-total AI adoption paired with profound anxiety/disorientation).
- **The Strategic Shift (From PI Panel):** We must move institutions from a defensive posture ("AI-proofing") to a proactive one ("AI-leading"). This requires escaping the "Gravity of the Boring" and leveraging the Seven-Step Strategic Framework to build capacity.
- **The Pragmatic Application (From Goodwill):** To make this tangible for the workforce, we must adopt a tiered model of AI implementation (Operational/Prompt Level, Workflow Automation, Systems).

## 2. Key Frameworks to Deploy

### A. The "Curatorial Turn" & New Roles (AAUA)

The transition from "sage on the stage" to "architect of discovery." For higher-ed leaders at the Lilly convening, emphasize how their faculty and staff must evolve into new roles to support workforce readiness:
- **Protocol Designers:** Faculty creating generative assignments (e.g., the "Un-cheatable Capstone") rather than static tests.
- **Generative System Architects:** Instructional designers building the workflows that teach "Systemic Thinking."
- **Disorientation Navigators:** Student success teams guiding students through the "Iceberg of Anxiety" and helping them find human meaning amidst automation.
